FAQ: Accounts & Profiles

How do I create an account and register my child for the CIS Summer Program?

Use our online registration platform, which is in many ways the same as last year's. Every family can create an account, and register as many children as they like under that one single account, including a single "running-total" invoice. Click the Register button up top to begin.

I registered last year and would like to keep my batch number and priority. What can I do?

Our system this year has been revamped. Because of the new way we process accounts, we did not keep last year's parent and student registration accounts for security and privacy purposes. Additionally, there will be no batch processing this year: parents will be able to create a family account and enroll children at the same time if they so choose. Changes can be made up until a certain deadline depending on the week in question, so it will be easier for everyone. You will require a new account, and there is no longer any need for priority batches!

I created my account but cannot find my confirmation email. What can I do?

To protect other users and yourself, our system will require you to validate the email address you give us. We do so by sending you a confirmation email which contains a link that you can click (or copy and paste into your web browser) that will automatically activate your account. Please check your spam and trash mailboxes in case our email is filtered by accident. If you cannot find it, please send us an email: our contact details are on the front page.

I forgot my email or password. How can I get help?

If you cannot remember your password, but know the email address you used during registration, please use the online Forgot Password function on our login page. If you cannot remember your email address, please call us: the details are on our home page.

I'm having trouble once logged in. What's the best way to get help?

In order to us to process as many help requests as possible, please use the online Help Centre once you've logged in. This will allow us to track issues quickly. All change requests must be made via the Help Centre.

Of course, if you still require assistance, or have general inquiries, please email us at the general address given on the home page.

FAQ: Enrollment

What is a PTE request and how do I submit one?

A PTE enrollment (short for Permission to Enroll) is an class registration given to your child by the Summer Program office based on particular situations, under which your child would normally not be able to register. For example, their birthday may be a few days or weeks off from a class that has some flexibility in age ranges - in this case, you would submit a PTE request if you like.

To do so, create a PTE ticket in the Help Centre. Our office staff will review your request and issue a response. PTE's are non-negotiable, as some courses, especially SCIT classes, may have strict age limits due to lab safety or other issues. Please understand that we are not able to PTE students under the minimum age of 5 years, as this is a school requirement.

How do I know whether my enrollments were successful and my children are on the class list?

You can view each of your children's confirmed schedules at any time, just by logging in and selecting their respective profiles. After submitting registration, enrollments are confirmed unless a payment is not made by the due date. Based on availability, your enrollments may be dropped without notice by the office if payment is not received on time!

What is the maximum class size and teacher-to-student ratio?

While teachers themselves select an appropriate maximum class size, we encourage all teachers to accept no more than eighteen students into each class. From previous experience, many classes are much smaller than that. In the 2012 Program, the teacher-student ratio was less than 1:8.

My plans have changed. How do I cancel or change my child's schedule?

You can cancel, change, add or remove courses to any of your children's schedule prior to the payment due date, or we receive payment. After the earlier of these dates, you should email or call our office to ensure that your request is processed promptly. Please note that late cancellation or change charges may apply.

FAQ: Payment and Billing

What are the accepted methods of payment?

We encourage all families to pay by self-reporting a mailed cheque (see below). When our office opens, you may also pay by cheque in person at our office, miscellaneous charges via cash by hand, or for families living outside Hong Kong, arrange a bank transfer (charges may apply).

At this time, CIS does not offer payment by credit/debit card, Internet payment, or money order. Please make crossed cheques payable to Chinese International School.

What is self-reporting payment?

To reduce delays in payment processing, we encourage you to use our new cheque payment format. Upon logging in to our website and going to your invoice, you may write a cheque for the amount of the invoice and enter some basic cheque information, including cheque number and value, securely into our system so we have a record that it will be coming soon. After submitting this information to our system, you will have five business days for our office to receive your cheque before the system automatically cancels this payment and marks it as invalid.

You will still have to mail in a physical cheque for security purposes, however, by giving us advance notice to expect your cheque, we will be able to process it much faster, which guarantees your children's enrollment and ensures you have no unpaid items.

May I make a special request to pay by bank transfer or credit card?

At this time, CIS unfortunately does not offer payment by credit/debit card, money order or traveler's cheques, and this is school policy that applies to both the Summer Program and regular day school. If you are an international family and do not have a Hong Kong bank account or cheques, please contact our office as soon as possible to arrange an electronic funds transfer.

What happens if my payment is late?

If full payment has not been made by the close of business (5pm Hong Kong time) of the due date, all of your children's courses for that due date will be temporarily dropped and will be available for enrollment by other parents. Upon payment, we will attempt to re-enroll your children as given in the schedule, however, we cannot guarantee that spaces have not been filled.

FAQ: Summer Program

More answers coming son. If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to email our secretaries!

Is the Summer Program open to all children?

Yes! The CIS Summer Program is, and always has been, open to CIS and non-CIS students aged 4-15. In the past we have catered to students from other international schools, local schools and from outside Hong Kong. If you'd like to find out more, please see our Program Details page. If your child is under 4, please contact us to see if your child is eligible for our younger programs. Please note that we cannot take every child under our minimum age due to safety and regulatory issues.

Do I have to sign up for all four periods/all four weeks?

No. Parents may sign up for as many periods and for as many weeks as they like. The same schedule in any one week is the same Monday thru Friday. We do require, however, that schedules must be a single continuous block of attendance: you will not be allowed to enroll a child whose schedule has period-long breaks. For example: you may not sign up for Periods 1, 2 and 4 while leaving Period 3 empty. You may, however, sign up for Periods 2, 3, and 4 only, leaving Period 1 empty.

FAQ: Daily Operations

What should I do if my child will be absent for the day (or for more than one period)?

We kindly ask all parents to email the office of all absences due to illness, outside engagements or other reasons. You may of course call us in addition, however, we do request a simple email as this will serve as a record and for safety reasons at school. Please also note that no refunds or make-up classes will be given for any absences.

My child is not from CIS. Will he/she get lost easily? How do I find my way around?

We've added a number of policies this year to ensure students never get lost at CIS. All our floors will be color-coded so it's easier for younger children to identify where they need to go. Additionally, our Office will be using the school's LCD screens on each floor to direct students, helpers and parents to the appropriate room - just like at the airport!

In addition, we are requiring teachers of younger age groups to bring the whole class back to the Office at the end of each period and collect the next period's students from there, so they will stay under adult supervision at all times.
